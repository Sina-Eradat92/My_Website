Biography:

 I grew up during a major technology boom,and begging my parents for a computer at the age of seven. As I got older my I continued to be curious about the world around me. I fondly remember working on cars with my dad, and wondering to my sell how the engine worked. Fast forward a few years to my first year in college studying biology. Trying to please my parents I found the subject to be interesting and boring all at the same time. After three years I finally took an introduction to programming call and my life changed forever. Programming opened the world to me, by showing me how the devises that I used ever day were controlled. In May 2018, I received my Bachelor's Degree in Computer Science from the California State University, Northridge.

 Since I was young I have always participated in sports and enjoyed being outside. The first sport that I competed in was basketball until I was thirteen. The summer before I went to high school I started swimming. I have always loved the water so the swim and Junior Lifeguard team at my local pool were a good fit. This led me to join my high school swim team where I competed at the varsity level for two years. When I first entered community college, I was able to join the school's water polo team as the goalie, and compete on the school's swim team in the 400 Individual Medally and the 200 Butterfly. Swimming also opened the door to me being a lifeguard and being able to mentor young teens at my local pool.

 I am creative and driven person who continues to grow and learn new skills every day. I am a very friendly person who believes in team work and supporting those around me. I enjoy learning and teaching because I truly believe that in order to master any craft you should be able to explain it to others that might be interested.
