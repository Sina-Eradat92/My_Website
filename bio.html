Biography

 I grew up during a major technological boom. At the young age of 7, I remember begging my parents for a computer. As I got older, my curiousity about the world around me only grew even more exponentially. I fondly remember working on cars with my dad, and wondering to my self about how the engine worked. Fast forwarding to my first year in college, I was an undergraduate student studying biology in an attempt to please my parents. I found the subject to be interesting and boring all at the same time. After three years, I finally took an introduction to programming class and my life changed forever. In May 2018, I received my Bachelor's Degree in Computer Science from the California State University at Northridge.

 Since I was a young boy, I have always participated in sports and enjoyed playing outside. The first sport that I competed in was basketball. The summer before I went to high school, I began my athletic career in swimming. I have always loved being in the water, so naturally, the swim and Junior Lifeguard team at my local pool were a perfect fit for me. My passion for swimming only grew as I got older. This led me to joining the high school swim team where I competed at the varsity level for two years. When I first began community college, I was joined the school's water polo team as the goalie. I competed on the school's swim team in the 400 Individual medley and the 200 butterfly. Swimming also gave me the chance to become a lifeguard, later on. As an aspiring future educator, my passion for mentoring young teens at my local pool really shined.

 I am a creative and driven person who continues to grow and learn new skills every day. I am extremely personable and love to meet and collaborate with others; whether it be for work or personal connections. When I entered university, I become a brother at the Alpha Epsilon Pi Fraternity. In AEPi, I learned teamwork, respect and what it meant to be a brother. I am a big believer in teamwork, supporting those around me and never taking the easy way out. I consider myself a lifelong learner. I truly believe that in order to master any craft, you should be able to attain the information in a way that can be passed down to someone else who is interested in learning.
